Ten‌ ‌teenagers‌ ‌have‌ ‌flown‌ ‌to‌ ‌Greenland‌ ‌for‌ ‌a‌ ‌ four-week‌ ‌Arctic‌ ‌expedition‌ ‌to‌ ‌show‌ ‌their‌ ‌headteacher‌ ‌that‌ ‌they‌ ‌are‌ ‌not‌ ‌a‌ ‌bunch‌ ‌of‌ ‌wusses.‌ ‌

Do‌ ‌you‌ ‌remember‌ ‌this‌ ‌awesome‌ ‌story?‌ ‌Back‌ ‌in‌ ‌2015,‌ ‌at‌ ‌‌Worksop‌ ‌College‌,‌ ‌in‌ ‌Nottinghamshire,‌ ‌England,‌‌ ‌‌a‌ ‌headteacher‌ ‌sent‌ ‌his‌ ‌pupils‌ ‌on‌ ‌an‌ ‌extremely‌ ‌difficult‌ ‌and‌ ‌dangerous‌ ‌Arctic‌ ‌expedition‌ ‌after‌ ‌saying‌ ‌that‌ ‌kids‌ ‌these‌ ‌days‌ ‌have‌ ‌become‌ ‌too‌ ‌soft‌ ‌and‌ ‌as‌ ‌he‌ ‌put‌ ‌it‌ ‌-‌ ‌"sanitized"‌ ‌because‌ ‌modern‌ ‌parents‌ ‌were‌ ‌too‌ ‌keen‌ ‌to‌ ‌protect‌ ‌them‌ ‌from‌ ‌everything.‌ ‌What?‌ ‌In‌ ‌response‌ ‌to‌ ‌this,‌ ‌ten‌ ‌brave‌ ‌teens‌ ‌decided‌ ‌to‌ ‌fly‌ ‌out‌ ‌to‌ ‌the‌ ‌remote‌ ‌Greenland‌ ‌wilderness‌ ‌on‌ ‌a‌ ‌World‌ ‌War‌ ‌II‌ ‌submarine-hunting‌ ‌seaplane‌ ‌where‌ ‌they‌ ‌got‌ ‌to‌ ‌face‌ ‌the‌ ‌dangers‌ ‌of‌ ‌polar‌ ‌bears‌ ‌and‌ ‌the‌ ‌extreme‌ ‌cold‌ ‌-‌ ‌which‌ ‌can‌ ‌cause‌ ‌frostbites‌ ‌and‌ ‌even‌ ‌hypothermia.‌

Their‌ ‌parents‌ ‌were‌ ‌extremely‌ ‌nervous‌ ‌about‌ ‌the‌ ‌trip.‌ ‌

The‌ ‌brave‌ ‌students,‌ ‌aged‌ ‌16‌ ‌to‌ ‌18,‌ ‌have‌ ‌spent‌ ‌one‌ ‌whole‌ ‌month‌ ‌in‌ ‌the‌ ‌Arctic‌ ‌wilderness‌ ‌-‌ ‌500‌ ‌kilometers‌ ‌away‌ ‌from‌ ‌civilization.‌ ‌

In‌ ‌that‌ ‌time,‌ ‌they‌ ‌have‌ ‌climbed‌ ‌three‌ ‌undiscovered‌ ‌mountains‌ ‌and‌ ‌carried‌ ‌out‌ ‌biodiversity‌ ‌fieldwork‌ ‌as‌ ‌well‌ ‌as‌ ‌training‌ ‌to‌ ‌get‌ ‌essential‌ ‌winter‌ ‌survival‌ ‌skills.‌ ‌

Throughout‌ ‌the‌ ‌expedition,‌ ‌the‌ ‌pupils‌ ‌carried‌ ‌all‌ ‌their‌ ‌equipment‌ ‌and‌ ‌supplies,‌ ‌and‌ ‌slept‌ ‌in‌ ‌tents‌ ‌or‌ ‌even‌ ‌just‌ ‌out‌ ‌in‌ ‌the‌ ‌open‌ ‌-‌ ‌keeping‌ ‌a‌ ‌watch‌ ‌for‌ ‌killer‌ ‌polar‌ ‌bears.‌ ‌What!‌ ‌They‌ ‌have‌ ‌spent‌ ‌an‌ ‌entire‌ ‌month‌ ‌of‌ ‌survival‌ ‌with‌ ‌no‌ ‌contact‌ ‌or‌ ‌support,‌ ‌except‌ ‌a‌ ‌camp‌ ‌doctor‌ ‌for‌ emergencies.‌ ‌

I‌ ‌don’t‌ ‌know‌ ‌about‌ ‌you,‌ ‌but‌ ‌I‌ ‌say‌ ‌they‌ ‌showed‌ ‌their‌ ‌headteacher‌ ‌what's‌ ‌what‌ ‌after‌ ‌he‌ ‌claimed‌ ‌that‌ ‌youngsters‌ ‌needed‌ ‌to‌ ‌be‌ ‌exposed‌ ‌to‌ ‌'genuine‌ ‌risk'.‌ ‌
